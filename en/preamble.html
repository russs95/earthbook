DO YOU like to have a glimpse of a book's background before you begin? Or perhaps you appreciate a quick summary before jump in? Well, either way, you're in luck.

In the culture of the Igorot people of Northern Luzon, story telling is central. In the stone plazas that are found at center of every village, stories are told to introduce people, lessons and ideas. It is an example, that we will follow in the pages ahead.

Banayan and I will begin with our own story-- how we came to meet and how this book was begun. We will then lay out the structure of the book and provide a short sentence, a long sentence and a paragraph that summarize the Tractatus Ayyew.

In Latin, the word tractatus refers to the philosophical tradition of a treatise— a systematic exposition of a particular concept.  In Kan’kan’ue, the language of Igorots, the word ayyew refers to the virtue of cyclic ecological integration that guides their culture. 

It is this ayyew ethos which inspires the theory of green and grey presented in the Tractatus Ayyew—   what we call an Earthen ethics.
