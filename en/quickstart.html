BEFORE A TREE BECOMES A TREE, its entirety is summed up in its seeds. In this spirit, we’ve put together a short sentence, a long sentence and a paragraph to define and describe the theory we will be laying out in the chapters ahead.

Let’s start with a short, one sentence definition of Earthen ethics:

That which follows the Earth’s example is green.

Now, here’s a longer sentence that defines green from an Earthen perspective. This one includes “must” to make it an ethic:

To be a green ecological contribution a process must follow the Earth’s pattern of concentrating matter and dispersing energy to enrich the vibrancy, diversity and consciousness of the system of which it is a part.

Now, let’s put our ethic within the broader context that we’ll be exploring:

As we become more aware of the ecological crises of our modern age, we long to amend and 'green' our ways. Our modern problem with plastic represents both the essence of modern crises and our longing to do better. As a fossil-fuel by-product, plastic embodies the planetary and human stories of both the biosphere's primordial flourishing and its modern depletion. By retracing plastic’s full multi-billion year story, not only can we understand the source of our plastic, we also can observe the ways in which the Earth systematically enriched the biosphere to enable our modern moment. In particular: the ways in which Earth’s spiraled its matter and energy inwards and outwards to unfurl ever greater vitality, diversity and consciousness. Observing societies that have long mastered their ecological integration we can see this same pattern embodied in their cultures. To follow their example, we must amend our metaphysics to recognize 'human ecological contribution'. Then, to achieve it (to be green) our processes require the embodiment of Earth’s pattern in the management of their energy and matter. Specifically, our enterprises must account and disclose their ecological impacts to show a net-dispersal of energy, a net-concentration of matter and a vitalization of the diversity and consciousness of the ecosystems that they are a part of. Finally, we can return to our plastic and embody the pattern of contribution into its management. This way, following Earth's example we can transition our plastic (and more) from a depletion to a contribution— from grey to green.

Our seeds sown, we now know where our roots are headed and how our branches will unfurl.
